iGeneralized Autoregressive Conditional Heteroskedasticity model with asymmetric leverage effects. Captures how oil price volatility clusters over time and responds differently to positive vs. negative shocks.

Brent Crude Price Forecast — Scenario Analysis

Forecast
i30-day scenario-based price projection. Escalation: Hormuz remains closed, war widens to Gulf infrastructure ($100-$120). Sustained Conflict: partial blockade, insurance-driven disruption ($90-$100). Ceasefire: diplomatic breakthrough, Hormuz reopens ($75-$80). Based on historical precedents (1990 Gulf War, 1979 Iran Revolution, 2019 Aramco attack).
Escalation
Hormuz closed, war widens to Gulf infrastructure. Oil supply disrupted by ~21 mbd. Historical parallel: 1990 Gulf War (+100%).
Target: $100 — $120/bbl
Sustained Conflict
Partial Hormuz blockade, insurance premiums deter transits. SPR releases partially offset. Slow grind higher.
Target: $90 — $100/bbl
Ceasefire / De-escalation
Diplomatic breakthrough, Hormuz reopens. Risk premium unwinds but remains elevated vs pre-war levels.
Target: $75 — $80/bbl
